# Hello, My Name is Adam Zhu

Undergraduate at **Carnegie Mellon University (CMU '29)** majoring in **Statistics and Machine Learning**
Passionate about applying data-driven thinking to real-world challenges — from **forensic science** to **computer vision**.
Interested in how analytics can make technology smarter, safer, and more human.

---

### Currently

- **SURF Research Intern**, National Institute of Standards and Technology (NIST) — building a machine-learning pipeline that separates bot from human traffic in NIST's public data portal logs (4 classifiers, F1 = 0.95) and mining the cleaned traffic for unmet user demand.
- **Building [TrueLine](https://github.com/adam-zhu1/trueline)** — an iOS app that turns a single iPhone into a bowling ball tracker: launch speed, breakpoint, and entry angle from video, via a fine-tuned YOLOv8 detector running on-device with Core ML. App Store launch in preparation.

### Previously

- **Research Intern**, Center for Statistics and Applications in Forensic Evidence (CSAFE) — camera-fingerprint device identification (24,000+ images; manuscript under review at *Forensic Sciences*) and degraded handwriting-similarity datasets.
- **Research Intern**, Virtual Reality Applications Center (VRAC), Iowa State — regression models of time-to-escape across 454 simulated-evacuation trials; co-author of a manuscript in preparation.
- **Co-Captain**, FIRST Robotics Team Neutrino #3928 (2021–2025) — led 7 sub-teams and 40+ members; multiple NASA Engineering Inspiration Awards and World Championship qualifications.
